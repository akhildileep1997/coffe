Types

  • Coffe beans are the seeds of a fruit called coffe cherry.Coffee cherries grow on coffee trees from a genus of plant called coffea.There are a variety of species of coffee plants,ranging from shrubs to trees


  • Type of bean:There are three types of coffee beans that are used in the production of marketable coffee. The names of these coffee beans are arabica, robusta and liberica, which are from the coffee plant genus Coffea arabica, Coffea canephora and Coffea liberica, respectively. Arabica is the most common of the three.


  • Type of Roast: There are four general categories of coffee roast: light, medium, medium-dark, and dark. However, these roasts break down to even more minuscule distinctions, which are a blast to explore if you're looking to expand your coffee palate. Here's your comprehensive roadmap to coffee roasts.

  • Type of Grind:The three main coffee grinds are coarse, medium, and fine, although each type has hybrid grinds. The different types of grinds depend on the type of brew you wish to make.The three types are Coarse grind,Extra coarse,Medium Coarse,Medium,Medium fine

  • Decaffeinated Coffe:Decaffeinated coffee has been made by removing the caffeine from regular coffee. Decaffeinated coffee is sometimes referred to as “decaf.”Decaf coffee is made from regular coffee beans that have been processed to remove most caffeine. The caffeine is extracted using one of three methods: water processing, solvent processing, or carbon dioxide (CO2) processing.

2) McCafe


McCafé is a coffee-house -style food and beverage chain, owned by McDonald's. Conceptualised and launched in Melbourne, Australia, in 1993, and introduced to the public with help from McDonald's CEO Charlie Bell and then-chairman and future CEO James Skinner, the chain reflects a consumer trend towards espresso coffees

3) Peet's Coffe


Peet's Coffee is a San Francisco Bay Area-based specialty coffee roaster and retailer owned by JAB Holding Company via JDE Peet's. Founded in 1966 by Alfred Peet in Berkeley, California, Peet's introduced the United States to its darker roasted Arabica coffee in blends including French roast and grades appropriate for espresso drinks.
